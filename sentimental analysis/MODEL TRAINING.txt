The hybrid architecture proposed underwent training in a modular fashion, with each expert model fine-tuned independently, followed by the subsequent ensembling process.

For the DistilBERT transformer, fine-tuning followed the procedure of Model Training #2 in section 4.2, with 800,000 tweets fine-tuned using the AdamW optimiser at a learning rate of 2×10⁻⁵ with mini-batches through three epochs, reaching a best validation accuracy of 86.56%.

Although the TF-IDF + Logistic Regression classifier and LSTM sequential model were fine-tuned on the entirety of tweets, they instead used 50,000 stratified samples to determine the final parameters, reaching validation accuracies of 78.56% and 74.49%, respectively.

Checkpoints of model training were saved at 10,000-step intervals to avoid losing progress or data to memory or power failure, and provide partial updates if retraining was required.

While the DistilBERT component of training showed trends of steadily increasing accuracy over the epochs, the validation curve did not similarly improve and eventually levelled out indicating that the model generalised well to the test data, with minimal overfitting.

Conclusion:

DistilBERT, Logistic Regression, and BiLSTM models are all integrated into a single weighted ensemble architecture in this study's hybrid sarcasm-aware sentiment analysis framework.  By skilfully combining lexical, contextual, and sequential representations, the suggested system makes it possible to fully comprehend the implicit and explicit emotions conveyed in social media text.  In the presence of ironic or unclear content, a specialised sarcasm detection module based on RoBERTa dynamically recalibrates sentiment polarity, further improving interpretability.  The ensemble model outperformed all individual submodels with an overall accuracy of 86.58%, according to experimental evaluation on the Sentiment140 dataset.  The confusion matrix's findings verified that both the positive and negative sentiment classes had balanced classification performance.
Furthermore, the system's resilience in managing intricate linguistic elements like emojis, slang, and sarcasm was confirmed by qualitative analysis using case studies.  All things considered, the suggested architecture creates a sentiment analysis solution that is scalable, interpretable, and context-aware and appropriate for real-time social media applications.  To improve its adaptability across a variety of contexts, future studies will concentrate on expanding this framework to multilingual sentiment detection and fine-grained emotion classification.
