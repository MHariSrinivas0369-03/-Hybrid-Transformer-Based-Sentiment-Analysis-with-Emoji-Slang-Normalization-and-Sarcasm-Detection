Data Collection and Preprocessing:

The experimental setup employs the Sentiment140 dataset, a sizable and diverse corpus of 1.6 million annotated tweets, with a total sampling record of 800,000 tweets (400 k positive + 400 k negative) selected for training and assessing the models. Each record consists of a short text message and a binary sentiment label derived automatically from user-provided emoticons. This dataset offers a realistic basis for examining informal social-media language that includes emojis, hashtags, abbreviations, and user mentions—all complexity barriers for traditional NLP (natural language processing) pipelines. The dataset was loaded directly from CSV, confirmed for class balance, and partitioned into training, validation, and test sets in a 90 : 10 : 10 ratio. Subsequently, 50 k tweets were sampled for classical and sequential models (TF-IDF + LR and LSTM) to optimize computation, while the complete 800 k corpus was used for fine-tuning the transformer.

1. Normalization of Language and Noise Removal 

The preprocessing pipeline initiates by lunching an automatic labor to reorder or remove raw tweets for detracting functional non-linguistic noise while maintaining the desired structure of any signal emitted from the tweets sentiment - positive, neutral or negative. The normalization procedure notes and sets all words to lowercase, removes URLs, usernames and punctuation by repetitive regular expressions, separates all hashtags into meaningful word shaking at the relative time required by the hashtags functional meaning, changes emoji into textual auspice using an emoji lexicon, uses a custom slang dictionary to expand abbreviations, e.g., 'luv' → 'love' and 'gr8' → 'great,' towards linguistic and through line representation. It even identifies polarity carrying stop words, e.g., 'not' and 'never’, to keep for the sake of preliminary resetting of polarity. The outcome of this procedure is normalized and sentiment preserved linguistics for hybrid modeling. 

2. Tokenization and Vectorization
Tokenization of the cleaned tweets are done according to specifications for the models. The DistilBERT tokenizer converts the text into subword tokens and attention masks, using padding or truncating to ensure all sequences to a standardized 128 tokens long or short. The Logistic Regression classifier and model use a TF-IDF vectorizer with unigrams and bigrams to encode a statistical importance of terms noted in context of the tweets from the tweets, while the LSTM uses a CountVectorizer  based on a 10,000 word vocabulary for indexing the tweets into sequences before embedding them. This  provides a consistent and fixed structure for input the dimensionality and features for the inputs in all the contextual-, statistical and sequence-based models.

3. Dataset for Hybrid Training
Three datasets were created for using the Sentiment140 dataset: the tensor model used the full 800,000-tweet dataset (learning rate 2 × 10⁻⁵, AdamW optimizer, 3 epochs, and batch size 8) and while the TF-IDF + LR models and LSTM were trained on 50,000 stratified samples each for five epochs each. The validated accuracies are 78.56% for TF-IDF + LR and 74.49% for LSTM. The model checkpoint, vectorizer and vocabulary1 was kept for inference, and the training in creating a hybrid system where training in a modular manner and parallel for hybridization in an ensemble system was successful.
